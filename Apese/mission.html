Impact Project

Youth

According to 2017 Turkana County projections, adolescents and youth aged 10–24 living with HIV accounted for 410,913 people, representing 39.3% of the county’s population. Adolescents (10–19) and young people (15–24) contributed to 25% and 47% of all new HIV infections respectively. High HIV rates among youth in Loima Sub-county are driven by extreme poverty (95%), limited access to sexual and reproductive health services, harmful practices like early and forced marriage, transactional and unprotected sex due to poverty, lack of sexuality education, intimate partner violence, and HIV-related stigma and discrimination—all of which hinder prevention and informed health-seeking behavior

HIV

Sexual and gender-based violence (SGBV) and human rights violations remain prevalent in many Kenyan communities, particularly in marginalized areas like Turkana. Contributing factors include low awareness of relevant laws, weak justice and health systems, punitive legal frameworks, inadequate legal aid, and limited programs addressing harmful cultural norms. Vulnerable groups such as adolescents, young people, and people living with HIV often face barriers to accessing services. SGBV, especially affecting women, is a major concern, with 14% of women and 6% of men aged 15–49 having experienced sexual violence. GBV includes physical, sexual, psychological, and economic harm rooted in socially constructed gender roles that shape unequal power dynamics and decision-making in society..
